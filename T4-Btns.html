BUTTONS SHOWCASE:


The primary action button stands out and represents the most important action on a page, such as submitting a form or making a purchase.

The secondary button is used for less important actions or as an alternative to the primary button. It's typically used for actions like canceling or going back.

The success button indicates a successful or positive action, such as completing a task successfully or confirming a successful operation.

The danger button warns users of potentially harmful or irreversible actions. It's commonly used for actions like deleting data or canceling a critical operation.

The warning button alerts users to potential issues or risks that need their attention. It's often used for actions that require caution or may have unexpected consequences.

The info button provides additional information or guidance to users. It's used for actions like displaying help messages or accessing additional resources.

The light button is subtle and non-intrusive. It's commonly used for secondary actions or as an alternative to more prominent buttons.

The dark button is used for actions that require a darker or more subdued appearance. It's often used in combination with lighter elements to create contrast.
